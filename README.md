Executive Summary
This project analyzes the economic impact of Post-Harvest Loss (PHL) in the Nigerian agricultural sector and provides a data-driven investment case for cold storage facilities. By bridging the gap between seasonal gluts and off-season demand, this model demonstrates how private investors can achieve high ROI while assisting the Federal Government in reducing its annual food import bill by an estimated 15–20%.

Key Insights & Analysis
Supply-Demand Arbitrage: A 12-month time-series analysis showing how cold storage can stabilize price volatility (e.g., preserving tomatoes bought at ₦15,000/basket during gluts to be sold at ₦45,000 during shortages).

Fiscal Impact Model: Quantifying the potential reduction in national food import dependency through domestic waste mitigation.

Facility Optimization: A geospatial recommendation for optimal cold-hub placement based on harvest volume and proximity to logistics corridors.

Tech Stack
Excel/Google Sheets: For the initial Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA) and ROI modeling.

SQL: To query and aggregate multi-year price datasets.

Tableau/Power BI: To visualize the "Profit vs. Waste" corridors.

Python: For seasonal trend forecasting.
